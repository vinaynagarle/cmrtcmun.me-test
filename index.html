Letter from the Director General

Honorable future delegates,esteemed colleagues and my dear friends,
-

It is my greatest joy and utmost pleasure to welcome you all for the second edition of CMRTCMUN, which will be held at CMR TECHNICAL Campus. This year's conference will take place from 8th February-9th February'19.

CMRTCMUN has provided a chance for young minds to gain awareness on current global conflicts, to lobby and debate on these issues in a public quorum, and to delve into the world of MUN. The conference brings together delegates of various countries to discuss and debate upon the problems of international importance and extensively, brainstorm solution for the better tomorrow. With all my faith, I have no doubt that this year’s edition will reach better standards.

As a Director-General, I take immense gratification to share that CMRTCMUN has always been a great motivational platform to work upon public speaking. With a diverse set of committees, we expect delegates to foster key skills such as research, public speaking, debate and negotiation.

We would also like to make a tribute to all of the secretariats, advisors, and sponsors for having assisted us in this Endeavour. Had it not been for the synergy and consummation with which we have worked together, CMRTCMUN would not have come thus far. The realization of each goal of this conference is due in part to your wondrous contribution.

We hope to organize the best CMRTCMUN to date, and ensure that everyone leaves with tons of new knowledge and improved skills, as well as countless memories and new friendships.

As the CMRTCMUN family, we are looking forward to see you in February. We hope that this conference will provide you with a broader perspective that helps you become more confident, knowledgeable and self-aware individuals. I hope that our months of hard work will exceed your expectations, and we will send you away with a memorable experience of a lifetime.

Best Regards,
VIKAS TRIPATHI
Director General.

MUN Committees


UNGA-SPE

United Nations General Assembly

The General Assembly is one of the six main organs of the United Nations, the only one in which all Member States have equal representation: one nation, one vote irrespective of the agenda. 193 Member States of the United Nations are represented in this unique forum to discuss and work together on a wide array of international issues covered by the UN Charter, such as development, peace, security and international law.

SAARC

SAARC, the South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation, was founded for soaring goals to promote welfare of south Asia and form a reliance among the countries of south Asia. Promoting the economic growth, social progress, cultural development , welfare of the people of South Asia and to promote their quality of life, understanding and appreciation of the problems of one another. Test your skills as you stand on your ground with common allies and enemies.

HRC

Address violations, promote human rights assistance and education, help develop international human rights law, review the human rights records of member States, work to prevent abuses, respond to emergencies, and serve as an international forum for dialogue on human rights issues. Peace and harmony is our strength and unity our purpose is to uphold human rights , our rights for all people in CMRTC MUN ’19.

UNSC

“War cannot be humanized it can be only abolished”
The Security Council has primary responsibility, under the United Nations Charter, for the maintenance of international peace and security. It is for the Security Council to determine when and where a UN Peacekeeping operation should be deployed. If you want to get past brainy actions with immediate consequences, UNSC at CMRTC MUN is the right place to be.
